# North Coast Laundry — Pricing & Unit Economics

**Launch context:** Owner-operated (2 people), no outside drivers yet.  
**Service area:** Arcata, Eureka, Loleta, Fields Landing (hub: Eureka / Arcata).

---

## Your real costs (per load)

| Cost | Low | Typical | High |
|------|-----|---------|------|
| Washer | $4.50 | $7.00 | $9.00 |
| Dryer | $1.50 | $2.25 | $3.00 |
| **Laundromat total** | **$6.00** | **~$9.25** | **$12.00** |

Plan at **$9.25/load** for wash + dry when budgeting.

**Also factor in per stop (not per load):**
- Gas + wear: ~$3–6 per round trip (depends on zone)
- Your time: pickup, return, folding, coordination

## What you keep (per load, full service @ $26)

| Line item | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Customer pays | $26.00 |
| Laundromat (wash + dry) | −$9.25 |
| Gas (allocated per load, batched route) | −$1.50 |
| **Gross margin before your time** | **~$15.25** |

If one order takes **~1 hour** of combined effort (2 people working in parallel counts as ~1 hour wall-clock with practice), that's **~$15/hr household** before taxes.

**How to make it worth it:**
1. **Batch by zone** — e.g. Tue Arcata, Thu Eureka, Fri south (Loleta / Fields Landing).
2. **2+ loads per stop** when possible (seniors, families).
3. **Cap daily stops** — 4–6 stops/day max at launch.
4. **Recurring pickups** — 10% off weekly/biweekly (predictable routes).

## Weekly targets (2-person team, launch)

| Metric | Conservative | Good week |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Stops/week | 12 | 20 |
| Avg loads/stop | 1.5 | 2 |
| Avg $/load | $26 | $27 |
| **Weekly revenue** | **~$468** | **~$1,080** |
| Est. laundromat | −$167 | −$387 |
| Est. gas | −$48 | −$80 |
| **Before your labor** | **~$253** | **~$613** |

At conservative volume you're learning the routes; at ~20 stops you're in side-income territory. Scale comes from density, not raising prices first.

---

## What NOT to do at launch

- Don't charge $10 pickup + $5/bag — **underpriced** vs $9+ mat cost alone.
- Don't go below **$22** on any delivered order.
- Don't take Fortuna / McKinleyville until routes are full in core zone.
- Don't add drivers until **you** can do 15+ stops/week reliably.

## Price review triggers

- Raise **$2/load** if laundromat costs stay above $10 average for 4 weeks.
- Raise zone fee to **$5** if gas/time on south runs hurts.
