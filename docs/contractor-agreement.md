# Independent Contractor Agreement — Humboldt Laundry Drivers

**DRAFT — Review with a California attorney before launch.**

## Parties

Humboldt Laundry ("Platform") and the undersigned individual ("Contractor").

## Relationship

Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee. Contractor controls when and whether to accept offers. No benefits, workers' compensation, or tax withholding provided by Platform.

## Services

Contractor agrees to:
- Pick up customer laundry during scheduled windows
- Wash, dry, and optionally fold garments per customer instructions
- Return laundry or make available for customer pickup
- Maintain separate, labeled loads per customer
- Use own equipment or licensed laundromat facilities

## Compensation

- Contractor receives percentage of order total as displayed in app (default 75%)
- Payouts via Stripe Connect (when enabled) or manual transfer during MVP
- Contractor responsible for all taxes (1099 issued annually if >$600)

## Insurance requirements

Contractor must maintain:
- Valid driver's license and auto insurance if transporting laundry
- Renters or homeowners liability coverage ($100k minimum recommended)
- Contractor acknowledges Platform does not provide insurance

## Background check

Contractor consents to background check. Platform may suspend access based on results.

## Quality standards

- No mixing customer loads
- Report incidents within 24 hours
- Maintain professional conduct with customers
- Follow Platform safety rules acknowledged during onboarding

## Termination

Either party may terminate at any time. Platform may suspend for safety violations, customer complaints, or failure to maintain insurance.

## Indemnification

Contractor indemnifies Platform against claims arising from Contractor's negligence, including garment damage and auto accidents during pickups.
